How learnerships work

A learnership is a 12–24 month programme combining classroom learning (20%) with on-the-job training (80%). You receive a monthly stipend from the employer, a nationally recognised NQF qualification at the end, and often a pathway to permanent employment. Grade 12 (Matric) is the minimum requirement for most learnerships.
